During emergencies, having food and water within reach is crucial because not only does it sustain our energy and hydration, but also prevents the trouble of looking for it. Additionally, it is advised that you pack food and water that will last 3 days and is easy to consume, such as canned goods.

üíä First Aid Kit


With a first aid kit, minor injuries will be prevented from getting worse from you and your loved ones, as it provides immediate relief in times of disaster. This kit generally contains items that aid such illnesses: hot and cold packs, sterile gauze, antibiotic ointment, throat lozenges, and paracetamol.

üî¶ Flashlight & Whistle


In times of disaster, having the ability to be seen and to be heard is just as important as being able to see and hear. With this, keeping a flashlight and whistle on hand can not only save your life, but also save someone else's.

üëö Extra Clothes


Having an extra set of clothes not only allows you to stay comfortable, but also keeps you protected from any harmful bacteria present during the disaster.
